# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Recruiters and hiring managers screening for Jan 2026 co-op candidates — typically with limited time, multiple tabs open, and a pattern-matching mindset. Secondary audience: senior engineers or technical leads evaluating a potential hire at the systems/fintech intersection. Both groups need to scan fast, assess fit quickly, and walk away with a clear impression of Luke's value.

## Product Purpose

A recruiter-facing dashboard that makes the case for hiring Luke Waehner. Not a portfolio in the "look at me" sense — a structured, efficient answer to: who is this person, what can they do, and why does it matter to us? Success looks like a recruiter spending under 60 seconds and leaving with a confident impression of technical depth, maturity, and a clear fit signal.

## Brand Personality

Confident, relaxed, clean. Luke knows what he's doing and doesn't need to prove it loudly. The design carries authority through restraint, not decoration. Selective flash — one or two moments that catch the eye — but never at the cost of information clarity.

## Anti-references

- Cluttered portfolio sites that bury the signal in animation noise
- Generic "template portfolio" aesthetics (hero + grid + icon rows)
- Overstyled glassmorphism or neon-on-dark looks
- Sites where you can't identify the candidate's actual skills in 10 seconds

## Design Principles

1. **The recruiter's first 10 seconds win or lose the hire.** Every above-the-fold decision optimizes for scan speed and immediate value clarity, not visual showmanship.
2. **Flash is earned, not sprinkled.** One or two high-craft moments (a transition, a data point, a layout beat) stand out precisely because everything around them is quiet.
3. **Technical credibility shows through specificity.** Real numbers, real stack names, real impact — not vague descriptions. The content is the proof; the design frames it without competing.
4. **Confident restraint.** Ample whitespace and a tight type hierarchy signal professionalism. Density signals desperation.
5. **One job per section.** Each scroll stop answers exactly one question for the recruiter. No section tries to do two things.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as baseline. No additional requirements.
